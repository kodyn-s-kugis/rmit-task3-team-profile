Allowing everyone in the group to review and collate the results of each individuals test is imperative to ensure from the team's inception that we work effectively.

If we look at the Learning Style test results, it shows the majority of the group have a visual approach; this would indicate that we would see a greater improvement of understanding when communicating in a visual context such as pictures or utilising chat records to refer back to info. However, in contrast to Chris, Kodyn and Matthew, Raymond utilised a different Learning Style test where it shows signs of potential difficulty in scheduling a consistent time to study or learn; this could pose a high degree of risk in coordinating information in a group assessment.

The behavioural tests are highly beneficial to understand in a group assessment context; this is because it provides everyone with significant insight into how someone might respond or act in a situation. Raymond and Chris showed similar results in the test, where they are primarily are driven on their inventiveness and creativity, but this also extends to their vigorous intellect and unique perspective. In contrast to Kodyn and Matthew, where they achieved similar behaviour results as well, but with some differences as well, they both are tailored to be leaders. The risk in the assessment primarily stems from having two strong leadership types; however, if we look deeper it shows Kodyn has a ruthless level of rationality, but when assessing Matthew's leadership behaviours it shows a stark contrast where he inspires others to achieve and perform well. Fundamentally both leadership types could complement each other in their weaknesses but still have the possibility to clash over the direction.

Overall we have to be careful when delivering an open conversation and ensuring differing perspectives are taken into account, whether or not they sound rationale, for example. The team will need to primarily focus on a visual communication type to support the majority of the team and maintain as much contact with members that have limited availability.
